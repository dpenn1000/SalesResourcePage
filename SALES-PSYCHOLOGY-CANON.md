# Sales Psychology Canon

*The shared message layer for CT Traditional training pages. Governs the recurring sales-psychology threads every training should reinforce, lightly and consistently, so the whole library speaks with one voice.*

---

## 0. Authority and pairing

This canon owns **message consistency**: the psychology threads, mantras, and DISC framing that recur across trainings.
It does **not** own structure (that is `TRAINING-AUTHORING-STANDARD.md`) or voice (that is `Voice_General.md`).
The three together are the full spec. Reference this file by pointer only, so it cannot drift.

These threads already appear across the trainings (cognitive-biases, know-your-buyer, door-resistance, the objection pages, credit-conversation, one-touch-close, plus the roleplay `objections-master.md`). The job is to reinforce them evenly, not to invent new ones.

## 1. The light-touch rule (read first)

Consistency of message, not a psychology lecture on every page.

- **At most 1 to 2 canon reinforcements per page,** woven into existing content. Never a bolted-on "psychology" section.
- **Stay on the page's topic.** Reinforce only the threads that serve what the page is actually teaching. A bill-anatomy page does not need the one-legger rule.
- **DISC only where reading the buyer is on-topic** (discovery, objections, language), and kept to a light nod, never a full DISC breakdown inside an unrelated page.
- **If it complicates the main point, cut it.** A focused page beats a complete one.

---

## 2. The mantras (reinforce broadly)

The strongest candidates: each already recurs on 2+ pages.

1. **Feel, Felt, Found.** Validate how they feel, share that others felt the same, tell them what those people found. Use the actual words. The universal empathy frame for any emotional hesitation.
2. **Don't argue, reframe.** Never defend the objection or the number. Shift the frame (price becomes a monthly comparison; a bad roof becomes a bundle).
3. **Make inaction visible.** Use the specific cost of delay on their own bill ("about $300 to $800 a month versus a locked rate"), never the vague "rates always go up."
4. **The one-legger rule.** Both decision-makers, or sell the present one the concept and lock a go-back. Roughly 80% cancel if you skip this.
5. **Read the room.** Stop pitching when they are settled, and verify ("did that answer your question?"). Reps talk themselves out of more sales than customers do.
6. **Be proactive.** Raise the hardest objection before they do ("everyone asks about snow"). It flips you from stranger to expert.
7. **"Not yet," not "no."** Frame setbacks (credit, roof, timing) as temporal, and always pair them with a dated path forward.
8. **Separate the person from the number.** A credit score or estimate is one formula's math, not a verdict on their worth. Say it out loud.
9. **Bundle, don't disqualify** *(solar and roofing only)*. A roof issue becomes one loan, one payment, one crew, not a reschedule. Pairs with leading with what we can do.

## 3. DISC (light nod, only where buyer-reading is on-topic)

Keep the letters and colors consistent. Naming varies across pages (`D/I/S/C` and `Driver/Visionary/Connector/Researcher`); they are the same system.

| Type | Color | Wants | Sell to them by |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D** Dominance / Driver | red `#E84040` | results, control | being direct, bottom-line first |
| **I** Influence / Expressive | amber `#F5A623` | the story, to feel special | bringing energy, then closing when excitement peaks |
| **S** Steadiness / Connector | green `#78C832` | safety, trust | rapport first; invite, don't pressure. "Let me think" and "check with my spouse" are trust shields |
| **C** Conscientiousness / Analytical | blue `#29A9E1` | data, proof | welcoming research questions; objections are legitimate questions, not obstacles |

## 4. Cognitive biases, used ethically

The governing line from the Cognitive Biases page: **communicate through a bias, never weaponize it.** Recognizing a bias is permission to present accurate information in a way the customer can actually receive, nothing more.

- **Loss aversion**: frame the cost of waiting, not just the gain.
- **Anchoring**: anchor on the cost of inaction first, so the solar number lands small by comparison.
- **Social proof**: cite installs in their area, not generic national stats.
- **Choice overload**: one strong recommendation, not a menu to shop.
- **Status quo and framing**: match a gain frame or a loss frame to the person.
- **Authority**: cite third-party data (DOE, codes), not company claims.
- **Scarcity**: genuine deadlines only. Manufactured scarcity destroys trust.

## 5. Empathy and objection-handling patterns

- **Feel, Felt, Found** for emotional hesitation (see mantra 1).
- **Following-questions rule**: ask the next question after the answer, and the next, until you actually understand. Don't move on after the first answer.
- **Hot potato**: let them answer their own question ("what have you heard?"). People believe what comes from their own mouth.
- **Short answer, then long answer**: punchline first, details second.
- **Diagnose the category before responding**: financial, decision-maker, roof or home, performance, timing, trust. Each has a different recovery path. Misdiagnosing the category is the most common closing mistake.

## 6. The one standardization to make everywhere

Across all pages, urgency must use the **specific** cost-of-delay number tied to the customer's actual bill. Retire the vague "rates always go up." This is the single thread pages tend to drift on.
