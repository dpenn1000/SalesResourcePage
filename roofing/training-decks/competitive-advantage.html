Part 7 · The Counterintuitive Truth

ArmourZone -- Why 4 Nails Beats 6

If a competitor's roof uses 6 nails per shingle and Dynasty uses 4, a homeowner's first instinct is that the competitor must be more secure. The opposite is true.

Same wind rating, fewer holes

Both Dynasty (4 nails) and most competitor shingles (6 nails) achieve 130 MPH wind rating. The difference is how they get there.

Dynasty's ArmourZone is a reinforced fabric band woven across each shingle in the nailing area. The reinforcement spreads load across a wider area, allowing fewer fasteners to do more work.

Fewer nails means fewer roof deck penetrations. Every nail hole is a potential leak point over 25 years. Dynasty puts 33% fewer holes in the deck and gets the same wind protection.

Competitor: 6 nails @ 130 MPH

More holes. Same wind rating. The extra two nails per shingle aren't adding security -- they're just more potential failure points in the deck over the life of the roof.

Dynasty: 4 nails @ 130 MPH

ArmourZone reinforcement does more with fewer fasteners. Same wind protection. Fewer penetrations. Cleaner deck. The original technology -- not a copy.

Talking-point flip: When a homeowner asks why we only use 4 nails, the answer is the strongest sentence in the deck -- "We're the only manufacturer that doesn't have to put 6 holes in your roof to hit the same wind rating."

Part 9 · Field Tactic

Competitive Quote Strategy

When a homeowner is shopping competing quotes, you don't have to win on price. You have to make sure they ask the same questions of the other roofer that they would ask of you. Five questions that change the comparison.

24/7 Service?
"Did you ask if their service calls are answered 24/7? Ours are."
Who's Doing the Work?
"Are they actually installing the roof, or subbing it out? Are they manufacturer-certified? Do they have a direct relationship with the manufacturer for distribution and warranty claims?"
Dedicated Service Team?
"Do they have dedicated service people, or do they show up only when they don't have paying installations scheduled?"
How Does a Claim Work?
"With Trinity, one call and we handle everything -- including filing with the manufacturer. What's their process? Do they hand you a phone number and walk away?"
Will That Warranty Last?
"If the owner is 55 today and gives you a 25-year warranty -- who honors it when he's 80? Trinity has been in business 32 years. We're not going anywhere."

Why this works

You're not asking the homeowner to take Trinity's word that we're better. You're handing them questions to ask the other roofer. Most local contractors can't honestly answer any of these in our favor. The homeowner discovers the gap themselves -- which is more persuasive than anything you could say about us.
