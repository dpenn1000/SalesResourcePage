Part 3

Know the Product

You can't sell what you don't understand. Know this cold before you knock a single door.

Solar 101

Solar panels convert sunlight into DC electricity. An inverter converts that to AC power, which your home uses exactly the same way it uses grid power. You don't own the panels on a PPA or lease -- you buy the power they produce at a fixed, lower rate.

Net Metering: Any excess energy your system produces gets fed back to the grid, and the utility credits your account for it. At night or on cloudy days, you pull from the grid and draw down that credit. The meter literally spins backward when you're producing more than you use.

Price Protection: On a PPA, the rate increases no more than 2.99% per year. Utility rates in New England have historically increased 5-8% annually. The gap widens every year in the customer's favor.

Financing Options

  • PPA (Power Purchase Agreement): Customer buys the power the system produces, not the system itself. $0 down, fixed rate, includes O&M. Most common product.
  • Cash Purchase: Customer owns the system outright. Higher upfront cost, best long-term return.
  • Loan: Customer owns the system, financed over time.

The 25-Year Warranty Package

  • System (panels + equipment): 25 years fully covered
  • Roof and workmanship (everything we touch during install): 25 years
  • Acts of God / natural disasters: covered
  • $1 million insurance umbrella over the property for incidentals
  • If the customer needs a new roof: Trinity's own roofing division coordinates the removal and reinstall -- no need to find a separate roofer

How to Bring It Up

You don't need to pitch roofing -- you need to ask one question and listen. The right moment is during your exterior walkthrough or discovery, while you're evaluating the roof for solar qualification.

"How are you feeling about your roof? Because if there's ever a time to look at it, it's before we put panels on -- and we can bundle it in a way that makes a lot of financial sense."

If they bite -- even a little -- transition: "Trinity actually has its own roofing division. Same company, same warranty umbrella. I can get you a quote while we're here."

Interest-Creating Remark

If you notice shingles that are worn, curling, or discolored -- say it out loud before they do. Don't wait for them to bring it up.

"I noticed a few of your shingles on the way in -- how old is the roof? I ask because we see a lot of homeowners realize halfway through the solar process that they should've addressed the roof first. We can do it all at once and save you a second mobilization."

Even if they say "the roof is fine," you've planted the seed. They'll remember it if something changes.

Qualifying a Home

Know these before you walk up. Most of the hard pre-quals you can see from the driveway.

🌳

Shading

  • Trees south of house must be below the roofline out to 60 ft
  • Home should be 2x as far from trees as trees are tall
  • Major factors: trees, chimneys, neighboring roofs, dormers, detached structures
  • If trees shade: is homeowner willing to remove?
🏠

Roof Pitch

  • Minimum: 6 degrees
  • Maximum: 45 degrees
  • Flat and A-frame roofs do not qualify
  • East-west facing homes: lower pitch is optimal
  • Best angle: due south
🧱

Roof Type

  • Eligible: Asphalt shingle, rolled asphalt, corrugated metal, Nescor metal, standing seam metal, rolled rubber (no foam layer)
  • Not eligible: Terracotta, slate tile, cedar shake
📐

Roof Capacity & Age

  • Panel size: 3.5 ft × 5 ft each
  • Enough unobstructed space for the design
  • Multiple dormers may disqualify
  • Roof must be 15 years old or newer
🏡

Homeownership

  • Must be on the title of the home
  • No renters -- period
  • Occupant must have lived there 2+ months
  • HOA: must have HOA permission and approval from current residents
⚡

Usage & Credit

  • Minimum electric bill: $50/month
  • System must produce at least 3kW
  • Minimum credit score: 650
  • Utility must have a working relationship with solar (most do)

Home Azimuth (Direction)

Due south is ideal. East-west facing homes can still qualify -- a lower roof pitch compensates by capturing more sunlight across the full day. This is not an automatic disqualifier; it's a design consideration.

Part 6

The Sales Process

There are two kinds of appointments: concept sales and closing sales. Know which one you're in and play it accordingly.

Concept Sale vs. Closing Sale

Concept Sale: The customer doesn't fully understand what solar is or how it works yet. Your job is education first. If you try to close before they understand the concept, you'll get objections you haven't earned the right to answer. Slow down, teach the utility comparison, walk through net metering, and get clear agreement at each step before moving forward.

Closing Sale: The customer already understands solar and has agreed it makes sense for them. Your job is to remove the remaining obstacles -- design, price, paperwork -- and get to the signature. Move with confidence and urgency.

The 10-Step Process

1

Pre-Appointment Preparation

Pull up the address, review the satellite image, check shading, note the roof type and direction. Design the system mentally before you walk up. Know the utility company. Check the bill if you have it. Never walk in cold.

2

Introduction & Rapport

Pay attention to appearance before you knock. Be confident and warm. Establish connection before you establish purpose. Listen more than you talk in the first 5 minutes.

3

Elevator Pitch

Short, sharp, positioning statement that earns you the seat. You are not selling solar yet -- you are selling the conversation. (See Pitches section.)

4

Discovery

Ask open-ended questions. Find out how they heat their home, how much they're paying, whether they've looked at solar before, what their long-term plans for the home are. Connect what you learn to rapport -- this is the same conversation, not a pivot.

Roof Injection Point

"How are you feeling about your roof? Because if there's ever a time to look at it, it's before we put panels on -- and we can bundle it in a way that makes a lot of financial sense."

Ask this every appointment, every time. It takes 5 seconds. A yes here turns a solar deal into a combo deal.

5

Utility Bill Review ("Kill the Bill")

Get the bill. Walk through it with them. Show them what they're paying for -- the distribution charges, the supply charges, the fees that never go away. Ask: "What if you could replace this with something that costs less and stays low?" Let the hurt sink in. This is the emotional turn in the presentation.

6

System Design

Show them what the system looks like on their roof. Walk through production vs. consumption. Make it visual and specific to their house -- not generic solar math.

7

Present the Numbers

Monthly savings. Rate comparison. 25-year projection. Referral earnings: $1,000 first install, $2,000 for every referral after that. Lead with value, not specs. They don't care how many panels; they care what it costs and what they save.

8

Handle Objections

Expect them. They are not rejection -- they are buying signals dressed up as hesitation. Acknowledge, validate, then answer directly. Never argue. (See Objections section.)

9

Close

Ask for the sale directly. "Does this make sense for your family?" If yes: "Then let's get the paperwork started." If they hesitate: go back to the objection. Don't skip this step. Don't assume. Ask.

10

Post-Sale Expectations & Referrals

Walk through the timeline, the notary process, and next steps before you leave. Set the communication expectation. Then ask for referrals -- every time, before you walk out the door. (See Post-Sale section.)

Probing Questions to Build Discovery

  • How long have you been in the home?
  • What does your electric bill look like -- high months, low months?
  • How do you heat the home? Oil, gas, electric?
  • Have you looked at solar before? What did you find?
  • Are you planning to stay in the home long-term?
  • Do you have an EV or are you thinking about one?
  • What would you do with the savings if your electric bill dropped significantly?

Self-Gen & Referrals Mentality

Leads are a reward, not a guarantee. The rep who builds a self-gen habit from day one never has a bad week because the pipeline dried up. Every door you knock is a potential lead. Every customer who installs is a referral source worth $1,000 per install. Work it every single day.

  • Ask for referrals at the close, at the notary, and when you check in after install -- first referral pays $1,000, every additional pays $2,000
  • Set your 10 personal contacts before you start taking company leads
  • Knock between appointments. Never sit in your car.
Part 7

Pitches

Two full in-home pitch frameworks. Study them both, understand the logic behind each one, and develop your own voice within the structure. Don't recite -- internalize.

Utility Framing

The Rachel Pitch

To start, what you are literally doing when you have solar is updating the electric utility for the house -- from power lines to solar panels. Right now you get power from the power lines. You don't own the power lines, but they attach to the house and you buy power from them, right?

Same thing for solar. You don't own the solar panels, but they attach to the house and you buy power from them instead -- as the new utility -- as long as that would be more cost effective. This costs nothing for you to have done, but it has to be more cost effective for a property to source from solar than from fossil fuels, or we can't do the project. That's what I'm here to figure out.

Ask: "Did that make sense?"

The house functions exactly the same as it does now. Electricity from solar panels comes into the house through the meter, just like power from the power lines does right now. Have you heard of net metering -- how you use and store solar power?

Explain net metering. Then:

The only thing that changes is the source of your power (the sun) and how you are billed for it. How does your electric bill look right now? Where's a copy we can look at together?

Transition to bill review.

The power of this pitch: it reframes the decision completely. The customer isn't being asked to "go solar." They're being asked to switch utilities to a cheaper one. That's a much easier yes.

Full In-Home

The George T. Pitch

So the way this works is we install the system on your roof at no cost to you. There's no out-of-pocket expense whatsoever. We don't charge for installation, we don't charge for the permit -- none of that. We make it really easy in the sense that there is no investment required on your end to get this done.

They'll ask: "Sounds great, but how is that possible?"

We get funded by the state and federal government -- the New Jersey State Clean Energy Program takes a little bit out of everybody's taxes each year for clean energy, and they use that to fund us to make it easier for homeowners to do this without paying anything. Because of the state funding, we're able to do this for you at no charge.

Once the system is installed, you get a 25-year warranty on the system itself -- fully covered. The roof and our workmanship are covered too. Everything we touch is covered for 25 years, including acts of God and natural disasters. And we carry a $1 million insurance umbrella over your whole property for incidentals. So it's not just the investment we take off your shoulders -- it's the risk. We're the ones liable, not you.

Once the system is installed and operational, you get three main financial benefits:

  • Monthly savings. Instead of continuing to pay a higher price to the utility every month -- which goes up quite a bit every year -- you lock in a lower price that stays low. We don't give you an extra expense. We just reduce a current expense you already have.
  • Price protection. The utility can go up 6%, 8%, 18%, 24%. They continuously lobby for higher rate hikes. What we do is not only drop you to a lower price, but keep you there. Your monthly price will never go up more than 2.99%.
  • Referral earnings. You earn $1,000 per referral -- any friend, family member, neighbor, or coworker you send my way who actually qualifies and gets installed. We have homeowners who earn more than they save.

Quick summary:

  • System goes up at no cost -- funded by state and federal programs
  • 25-year warranties: system, roof, workmanship, acts of God
  • Monthly price drops and stays low (never more than 2.99% annual increase)
  • $1,000 for your first installed referral, $2,000 for every one after that

Pause. "I know that was a lot -- any questions so far?"

This pitch is comprehensive and covers most objections before they come up. It's best used with customers who need the full picture before they'll engage -- typically analytical or dominant personality types. For relational customers, use the Rachel framework and slow down on discovery first.

Part 8

Objection Handling

An objection is not a no. It is a question that hasn't been answered yet. Know these responses cold.

At the Door (Upfront Objections)

"I'm all set" / "I'm not interested"
"No worries -- I'm not looking for any commitments today. I'm just letting you know that this house would qualify. The reason so many people are jumping on board is..."
"We already looked into solar and the savings weren't much"
"Okay, what interested you in solar originally? This home may have a different outcome than what you saw before."
"I don't like the way it looks"
"Have you noticed how much more solar is going up in the last couple of years? I think in the next few years, it'll be the houses that didn't qualify that stand out."
"I'm too old for solar"
"If the utility company came to you tomorrow and offered you a better price, would you say no? If that power came from a cleaner source, would you be opposed?"
"We want to wait for newer technology"
"Solar technology is completely different from consumer electronics -- it doesn't change at the same rate. Solar panels have been around since 1951, and efficiency is only increasing about 2-3% a year. The only thing people are doing by waiting is throwing money at the utility companies."

In-Home Objections

"What happens if I sell my home?"
"Trinity has a 100% transfer rate to the new homeowner. Solar homes tend to sell faster and can sell for a higher price."
"What happens if I need a new roof?"
"If you need a new roof, we've got you covered -- Trinity has its own roofing division, so we coordinate everything ourselves. No need to find a separate roofer. And panels actually extend the life of your shingles -- the shingles are safer under the panels than exposed."
"Will the panels hurt my roof?"
"A panel system weighs less than an additional layer of shingles -- about 3 lbs. per square foot. The system actually makes the roof more structurally stable: hurricane, wind, rain, and snow resistant."
"I've read bad reviews about solar / about Trinity"
"When you're researching reviews, look for sites that aren't pay-to-play -- companies can buy better reviews on a lot of platforms. The only site I trust is the BBB because it's regulated. Trinity has had an A+ rating there for years."
"I need to talk to my spouse / partner first"
Don't leave. Ask: "That makes complete sense -- is there any way to get them on the phone right now, even for 5 minutes? I want to make sure they have the same information you do so you can make this decision together." If they truly can't: set a firm go-back appointment before you leave, with a specific time and both decision-makers present.
Part 9

Post-Sale Expectations

What you say after the close determines how clean the install goes and whether you get referrals. Do this every time.

Setting Expectations Script

Walk through this before you leave -- every single time.

Timeline: "Expect the process to take about 90 days to get the system to the roof. Installation itself typically takes about one day per 5kW of system size. After install, it takes another 30-45 days to button up everything with the utility. So all in, you're looking at about 150 days from today to a live system."

Communication: "Throughout the process, I need you to communicate with me. If you have any questions -- about anything -- call me directly. Most importantly, when you get a call about your notary appointment, set it immediately. That's one of the biggest factors in the timeline. As soon as you hear from us about the notary, schedule it. That will do more than anything else to move things along."

Best contact method: "What's the best way to reach you -- text or call?" (Recommend text -- people respond faster.) "Be on the lookout for a text or call about your notary. We'll plan for it in about 30 days, but if we can move it up, we will."

Before you leave: Log the expected notary date in Salesforce. Note it in your sales notes. If anything changes on the customer's end, they need to reach out to you directly.

After the Sale: Rep Checklist

  • Deal submitted in Salesforce before end of day
  • Box/PDF sync completed -- all documents uploaded
  • Expected notary date logged in sales notes
  • Customer communication preference noted (text vs. call)
  • Referral ask made before you left the house
  • Follow-up scheduled in your calendar for 2 weeks out

The Referral Ask (At Close)

Don't wait until install. Ask the moment the paperwork is signed, while excitement is highest.

"Congratulations -- you're going to love this. Real quick, before I head out: do you have any friends, family, or neighbors who own their home and have a decent electric bill? Your first referral who gets installed pays you $1,000. After that, every single one pays $2,000. Who comes to mind?"
