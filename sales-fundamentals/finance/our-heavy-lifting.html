Trinity Solar

Our side of the deal

It's a 25-year agreement.
Here's everything we carry.

Going solar with us isn't a hope or a handshake. It's a long, written commitment. Here's the part people don't always realize: almost all of that commitment is ours. We design the system, install it, own it, insure it, repair it, and guarantee it produces. You buy the power. That's the deal.

"Wait, what do you actually do?"

Almost everything. Here it is, all six big pieces, in plain English. No fine print, nothing hiding behind it.

1

We install the system, and we own it.

The panels go on your roof, but the equipment is ours. There's nothing for you to buy, finance, or maintain. We own and operate the whole system for the full 25 years.

2

You pay nothing upfront.

No deposit, no equipment cost, no install fee. Nothing at signing. Nothing on install day. You start paying only after the system is on and generating power for you.

3

Your rate is locked. Your bill is predictable.

You buy power from the system at a rate we agree to in writing. Your rate and your monthly bill move on a schedule that's set the day you sign. No utility surprises.

4

If it underproduces, we pay you.

Our production guarantee. If the system ever makes less power than we promised, we credit you the difference at your rate. That's written into the agreement, every year, for the whole term.

5

We warranty, insure, and repair the system.

Free, for 25 years. If anything breaks, we fix it. If anything gets damaged, that's on us. The system is insured separately, so it doesn't ride on your homeowner's policy and your premiums aren't affected. You never see a repair bill.

6

We handle all the red tape.

Permits, engineering, electrical and structural reviews, utility approvals, every code requirement, every form. You don't track any of it. We do the work, you stay out of the paperwork.

Twenty-five years of design, install, ownership, insurance, repair, and a written guarantee. We handle the heavy lifting. You handle a few simple things. That's the deal, and it's a good one.

Questions about anything here? Ask. We'd rather over-explain than leave you wondering.
