Trinity Solar

The honest conversation

Solar isn't magic.
Here's the honest version.

If a deal sounds too good to be true, you should ask questions. So let's be straight with you. Solar will lower what you spend on power, but it isn't free and it isn't magic. It's a fair trade: we take on the hard, expensive part, and in return we count on you for a few simple, reasonable things.

"Okay, so what's the catch?"

Honestly, there isn't one hiding in the fine print. There's just your side of a normal agreement. Here it is, all six pieces, in plain English.

1

Take care of your home, the way you already do.

Your panels will sit on your roof for decades. Keep your home in good shape like any owner does, and they'll keep doing their job. Going solar doesn't change the fact that you own and care for your house.

2

Yes, you still have a monthly payment.

Solar isn't free electricity. It trades a large, unpredictable utility bill for a smaller, steadier solar payment. Please pay it on time, the same way you always have.

3

Let us in when your system needs attention.

It's rare, but if your system ever needs service, our team has to be able to reach it. Just give us access to your property when we ask.

4

Birds, bees, and squirrels are still your call.

Same as today: pests are part of owning a home. If something tries to nest near your panels, handle it the way you would anywhere else on your property. (Ask us about critter guards if you'd like them.)

5

Please stay off the roof.

Don't climb up to clean or repair anything yourself. It isn't safe, and it's genuinely our job. If something looks wrong, call us and we'll take care of it.

6

Stay in touch while we get you running.

Going solar takes a few visits, a few signatures, and a couple of inspections. The quicker you respond when we reach out, the quicker your system is on and saving you money.

That's the entire list. No magic, no mystery, no surprise fees buried on page nine. We handle the heavy lifting. You handle these few simple things. That's the deal, and it's a good one.

Questions about anything here? Ask. We'd rather over-explain than leave you wondering.
