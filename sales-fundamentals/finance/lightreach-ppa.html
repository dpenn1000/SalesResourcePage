Closing · Rep Mastery

Walk the LightReach PPA

Know this agreement like the back of your hand. Plant every key point during the consultation, and the signing becomes what it should be: putting in writing what you already talked about.

The idea

Mastery is what kills hesitation

You learn all 36 pages cold not so you can recite them, but so you never stall when a homeowner pauses. By signing, trust is already built. Most customers won't scrutinize every line. Keep it simple, keep moving, and be ready to explain any section in one clean breath the moment they stop on it.

The agreement is where we put everything we talked about in writing. When the customer recognizes every point because you planted it earlier, you've already won.

Default speed

Light, and moving

Name the section, tie it to what you already covered, keep going. This is how you run most of the page.

On-demand speed

Instant, when they stop

If they pause on something, explain it clearly and confidently, then move on. Hesitation is the only real failure here.

Section library

The rest of the document, one card each

Behind the first page, the body sections and exhibits are mostly the legal long-form of what you already covered. Run them at default speed. Each card gives you the one line to say, plus what to have ready if they stop.

PPA Section 3

System & Additional Products

"This confirms the system we designed, the size and equipment. Initial right here."

If they stop
  • Approved-manufacturer list, with substitution rights, but you're guaranteed the performance.
  • Battery (rising with the Construct 5 ESS incentive): backs up designated circuits only, holds a 20% reserve, not guaranteed for every outage, never for life-support.
First initials. Show them exactly where, every time.
PPA Section 4

Payments

"$0 deposit, it comes out automatically each month, and here are a couple of practical things."

If they stop
  • Set up autopay, or it's $15 more a month.
  • Tax credits belong to the owner. Standard for a product you don't own.
  • Buyout at fair market value from year 5. Prepay at a 5% discount.
Autopay is the one to land proactively.
PPA Section 5

Your Obligations

"This is the detailed version of that short list. Same deal, just the legal language."

If they stop
  • Roof and structural integrity is the homeowner's, settle this in the driveway, not here.
  • Keep trees trimmed so production holds.
  • Data sharing runs the system and proves the guarantee.
The roof opens with "How do you feel about your roof?" outside. See the Roofing module.
PPA Sections 8 & 10

Transfer & Selling Your Home

"If you ever sell, you've got options built in. Usually the buyer just takes it over."

If they stop
  • Buyer assumes the PPA with a quick credit check.
  • Or buy the system and sell the home with it owned.
Already handled in the consult. Full detail lives in the "If I Sell My Home?" objection card.
PPA Section 9

Ownership & Tax Credits

"This says LightReach owns the system and the incentives that come with it. That ownership is why we carry all the cost and risk, not you."

If they stop
  • Any credits or incentives, now or later, belong to the owner.
Reframe: ownership is the engine of their whole deal, not a loss.
Exhibit G

Performance Guarantee

"Your production number is confirmed by our Remote Assessment Team. They use advanced design tools and stay conservative when they analyze your site. The guarantee is your written protection in the unlikely event something falls short."

If they stop
  • Every 3 years we true up actual vs. guaranteed and credit any shortfall at your rate.
  • Surplus banks forward. Extra production is yours, free.
  • Hail is covered. A tree you let shade it is not.
Two protections, same direction: the estimate is built to be beaten, the guarantee sits under it for the rare miss.
PPA Sections 7, 11 & 12

Warranty, Loss & Liability

"The system is warrantied, insured, and repaired at no cost for the full 25 years. If it breaks, we fix it. You never see a repair bill."

If they stop
  • Insured separately, not on their homeowner's policy.
  • Coverage assumes they hold up the simple obligations.
Land the separate insurance. It removes a worry they didn't know they had.
PPA Section 26

Right to Cancel

"You've got 10 days to cancel for any reason. Full refund, no questions. If anything doesn't sit right after we leave, you're covered."

If they stop
  • Written notice before midnight of the 10th day, email or the Exhibit C form.
  • Confirm no blank spaces, and that they get a complete copy.
A reversible decision is an easier decision. See the no-cancellation framing below.
Where strong reps diverge

The no-cancellation framing

The 10-day right is real and you honor it out loud. Then you give the customer a deliberate moment to confirm they're ready, before either side starts spending.

Say it like this

"You do have a 10-day right to cancel, and Trinity stands behind it. If a real emergency forces your hand, nobody's going to charge you. But personally, I run a no-cancellation policy, and here's why. The moment I leave tonight, we go to work for you. Applications, engineering, permits, electrical and structural reviews, utility approvals. We start spending money on your project right away. So if any part of you is thinking you might want to cancel, I'd rather we stop right here and work through it. What's left that we haven't talked about?"

"And understand how this works from here. This is a qualification process. Signing tonight doesn't mean you're approved. You're not fully approved until your installation actually starts. So let's make sure everything's solid before I take this back and put the team on it."

Why it works

  • It's your best shot at hidden objections. A quietly unsure customer nods, signs, and cancels three days later. This pulls the doubt out while you can still handle it.
  • The takeaway flips the frame. They stop feeling sold and start feeling like they have to qualify.
  • The work-starts-now point is true, so it lands. Name the specific work.

Integrity, not pressure (keep this visible)

This pause exists for the customer. It's a deliberate checkpoint to confirm they're truly committed and that nothing's been left unaddressed before either side invests. You're handing them a clean moment to say "wait, I'm not sure," which is the opposite of forcing a signature through. You honor the 10-day right out loud, every time. The framing is about commitment and surfacing concerns, never a claim that they can't cancel. A customer who hears it this way feels respected, not cornered.

The close

Make the signature a win

If the consultation did its job, the signature is anticlimactic in the best way. They're nodding, not deliberating. Lead them to every signature and initial, confirm no blank spaces, make sure they leave with a complete copy.

Congratulations

"You just locked in your power for the next 25 years, and you did it without spending a dime today. Welcome to the Trinity family. Let's grab a photo, you'll want to remember this one."

A genuine celebration reinforces the decision and starves buyer's remorse before the 10-day window. Mean it. Keep it to a line or two, no performed excitement. Then flow into the photo and saving each other's contacts.
