How Your Pay Works: Commissions and the Sales Clubs

Your commission isn't a flat rate. It's a formula you can move. Hit a Sales Club and three levers shift in your favor at once, on every deal you write for the year. Learn the math, then learn the path to the next tier.

Module 1

Your Pay Structure Rewards Skill

Most pay plans pay the same on deal one and deal one hundred. Yours doesn't. The better you get, the more every single deal pays, because a Sales Club is a raise you earn once and keep all year.

A club is a standing raise

Clubs aren't a one-time bonus. Qualify, and a higher rate rides on every deal you write for the year. Skill compounds.

Volume and quality both count

The clubs reward installs and active contracts, across solar, roof, and battery. You build the number with real, clean deals that stick.

The math is knowable

Every check comes from one formula. Once you can read it, you can see exactly what the next tier adds to each deal.

The frame for this deck: not "work harder for the same pay." It's "get to the next club, and the raise follows you on every deal." We'll show the dollars.
Ask the room: who here knows which club they're tracking toward right now? If the answer is "no one," that's the gap we close today.
Why is a Sales Club called a raise you keep, not a bonus?

AnswerA bonus pays once. A club shifts your commission formula for the whole year, so a higher rate rides on every deal you write. The skill that earned it keeps paying.

Module 2

Anatomy of a Commission: The Four Parts

Every per-job commission is four moving pieces. We'll walk a sample 8.2 kW deal so the numbers are concrete. Treat the dollars as illustrative, the structure as exact.

  1. 1. Base Payout

    Base EPC times your base rate. Base EPC is system kW times 1000 times the base price per watt (3.00). The rate is 4% on a company or Outreach lead, 2% on a self-gen.

  2. 2. Lead Fee

    Subtracted from the base. Outside a club it's 475 per deal. Your club tier shrinks it.

  3. 3. Above-Base Adder

    Pays you on price above base. The system EPC above base EPC, times your above-base rate. Sell above the floor and this grows.

  4. 4. Club Adder

    A percentage of club value, added on top once you're in a tier. High Flyer 0.5%, President 0.75%, Royalty 1.0%.

The formula, in one line: Base Payout (Base EPC times your rate) minus Lead Fee, plus Above-Base Adder, plus Club Adder. Want to model your own deal? Use the Commission Calculator.
The floor: minimum commission is 750 per deal. If the price per watt comes in below base, the deal pays the floor instead of the formula.
Ask the room: in the four-part formula, which part does a rep actually control in the living room? (The above-base adder, by where they price the deal.)
Name the four parts of a per-job commission.

AnswerBase Payout (Base EPC times your rate), minus the Lead Fee, plus the Above-Base Adder (price above base times the above-base rate), plus the Club Adder. A 750 floor catches any deal priced below base.

Module 3

The Three Levers a Club Moves

One qualification moves three numbers at the same time. Your above-base rate climbs, your lead fee drops, and a club adder appears. Here's before and after on the sample 8.2 kW company-lead deal.

No Club

  • Above-base rate: 25%
  • Lead fee: 475 per deal
  • Club adder: none

High Flyer (first tier)

  • Above-base rate: 33%
  • Lead fee: 375 per deal
  • Club adder: 0.5% of club value
25% → 33% Above-base rate climbs
475 → 375 Lead fee drops per deal
+0.5% Club adder appears
Net on the sample deal: moving from No Club to High Flyer is worth about 557 more per job. Three small shifts stack into one real number, on every deal. Figures are illustrative, not a promise.
Ask the room: three levers move at once. Which one surprises reps most? (Usually the lead fee, because they never see it as a line they can shrink.)
What three things change the moment you hit a club?

AnswerThe above-base rate rises (25% to 33% at High Flyer), the lead fee drops (475 to 375), and a club adder is added (0.5% of club value at High Flyer). All three at once, on every deal.

Module 4 · Elite Club Metrics

The Sales Clubs and How You Qualify

Three tiers, earned over the year by annual counts. Installs and Active Contracts each include Solar, Roof (to include RR&R), and Battery. One gate applies to all three.

High Flyer

Tier 2

President's Club

  • Installs25 to 34
  • Active contracts30 to 39

Tier 3 · Top

Royalty Club

  • Installs35+
  • Active contracts40+
  • Club adder1.0%
The gate: your Solar active-contracts rep-control percentage must be 20.00% or lower to qualify for any tier. Hit the counts and miss the gate, and you don't make the club. Protect that number all year.
Ask the room: counts get you to the door, the under-20% gate lets you in. Whose rep-control number do we need to watch this quarter?
You hit 26 installs and 31 active contracts. Which club, and what could still stop you?

AnswerThat's President's Club range (installs 25 to 34, active contracts 30 to 39). One thing still stops you: a Solar rep-control percentage above 20.00%. Clear the counts and the gate to qualify.

Module 6

The Strategy to Get There

The clubs ask for two numbers, installs and active contracts, with a gate. Each one has a play. Here's how to build the count without gaming it.

Active contracts vs. installs

Active contracts run ahead of installs in every tier. Keep deals clean and on track so contracts hold active and convert to installs, not cancel.

Use the full product line

Roof and battery count too, alongside solar. A bundle that solves the whole home builds your number and serves the customer. Don't disqualify a roof, fold it in.

Protect your rep-control percentage

The under-20% gate is yours to lose. Watch it all year. Hitting the counts means nothing if the gate closes on the rate you control.

The whole-home path: since roof and battery both count, the rep who sells the whole home climbs faster than the solar-only rep at the same door count. Bundle, don't disqualify.
Track it live: the official commission and club tracking dashboard for reps and managers is the Trinity Sales Commission Dashboard. Check where you stand against each tier.
Ask the room: each rep names their gap to the next tier in two numbers: installs needed and active contracts needed. Then name the one thing this week that closes it.
Roof and battery count toward the clubs. What does that change about your strategy?

AnswerSelling the whole home builds your install and active-contract count faster than solar alone. A roof or battery becomes part of the number, not a side deal. Bundle the home, climb the tiers.
