## 1. The core idea

Community partner events are charity-aligned tabling events (YMCA, scouting, fairs, "send a kid to camp"). Trinity sets up a table at an event the organization is already running. The homeowner's goodwill toward the charity is the warmth in the lead. These are **not** canvassed leads and must not be worked like canvassed leads.

Why they are different from every other lead source:
- **No windshield time.** People come to you at the table.
- **Warm by association.** They already trust the organization hosting the event.
- **Two motives in the room.** Some homeowners are there because they *care about the organization*; some are there to *save money*. The rep's job is to read which one they are and close on that motive (Michael Garcia, ~43:30--44:16). If they love the org, the appointment is the easy part. If they're money-driven, lead with savings.

## 2. The flow, end to end (canonical sequence)

This is the reconciled, no-contradiction version. Every page follows this order.

1. **At the table -- earn the conversation.** Engage warmly, tie to the charity, read the motive (cause vs. savings).
2. **At the table -- SET the appointment in person.** Not "we'll reach out." The rep names a specific day and time and gets a yes, on the spot, with the decision-maker(s) confirmed present. (Anthony's live demo, ~31:20--33:10: *"Are you available Thursday, 6 o'clock? ... Your wife's available? What's your wife's name?"* And Dan, ~09:48: the homeowner *"looked in your eye and said ... meet with me next Tuesday at three."*) **The time is set at the table. This is the load-bearing moment.**
3. **At the table -- exchange numbers, homeowner texts the rep.** Rep gives their number; homeowner sends a text right then so the rep's number is saved in the homeowner's phone and the rep has the homeowner's number from a real send. (See contradiction #1 resolution below.)
4. **Within a day -- the rep sends a confirming text.** Warm, human, short. It **confirms the appointment that already exists** -- it does not hunt for availability. (See contradiction #2 resolution below.)
5. **The rep locks it on the schedule themselves** and marks it confirmed so the inside/call-center team does not also call the homeowner (avoids the double-call). The rep is the fail-safe; the rep owns the outcome.

## 3. Contradictions found in the transcript -- and how they resolve

### Contradiction #1 -- Who texts first at the table?
- **Anthony's version:** the rep texts the homeowner the details and gets a thumbs-up back.
- **Dan's version (chosen earlier in this project):** the homeowner texts the rep first, so the rep's number lands in the homeowner's phone and the homeowner has taken a small action that raises commitment.
- **RESOLUTION (ships):** **Homeowner texts the rep first**, at the table. Rep says some version of *"Do me a favor, grab your phone -- here's my number, shoot me a quick text right now so we've both got it."* This is what's already in Stage 4 of the page and it stays. Rationale: micro-commitment + guarantees the rep's number is saved under a real contact.

### Contradiction #2 -- What is the follow-up text FOR?
- **The wrong version (currently live in Step 2, must be fixed):** the text gathers availability -- "what works better this week, I've got Wednesday at 2 or Friday at 4." This treats the appointment as unset.
- **The correct version (per the table-set principle and Dan's explicit correction):** the appointment time is **already set at the table.** The follow-up text only **confirms** it and keeps the relationship warm. It is a reassurance/anchor message, not a scheduling negotiation.
- **RESOLUTION (ships):** Step 2 becomes a **confirming** text. Example voice: *"Hey [name], great meeting you at the [event] -- looking forward to [Day] at [time]. I've got you on my calendar. Any questions before then, just text me here."* No menu of windows. No "are you free." The time exists; the text protects it.

### Contradiction #3 -- Corporate event instructions vs. the rep-driven follow-up
- **The tension:** official corporate event instructions deliberately **exclude** "take pictures, text them, follow up, call them" because corporate "doesn't want to be overly aggressive when it comes to communications" (~01:08:00 region, lines ~4095--4184).
- **The brainstorm's position:** the rep's personal, warm, one-to-one follow-up is exactly what converts these warm leads, and is different in kind from aggressive mass outreach.
- **RESOLUTION (ships):** Frame the rep follow-up as **relationship maintenance the rep personally owns**, not a corporate communications campaign. One warm human text from a number the homeowner already saved is not "aggressive outreach." Keep it personal, single-channel (text), and tied to an appointment the homeowner agreed to in person. Do **not** present this as automated/blast follow-up. This keeps it on the right side of the corporate concern while still capturing the conversion the team wants. If a page ever needs to cite policy, it says: the rep confirms an agreed appointment; it does not cold-pursue.

## 4. Supporting ideas worth keeping (from across the transcript)

- **Reps earn the right to run events.** Events are a privilege/reward for reps who are performing; not everyone is staffed onto them (scheduling discussion, ~45:00--46:20). This protects the partner relationship.
- **Mark the appointment "confirmed" in the system** so the call center does not double-call the homeowner. Rep-owned scheduling is the point.
- **Same-day bill capture.** Ask the homeowner to text their utility bill the same day while goodwill is highest, so the appointment is workable before it's even sat.
- **Existing-solar homeowners are not dead.** Pivot to a second system, battery, or roof.
- **Read the motive and close on it** (Michael Garcia): cause-driven vs. savings-driven homeowners need different closes.
- **No-shows / flake risk is lower but real.** Because there's no windshield time and the lead is warm, the confirming text + self-scheduling is what protects the sit.
- **Role-play the table** (Michael Garcia's suggestion, ~48:16): practice people walking past the table and the rep pulling them in. Good future training asset.
- **Call parties / targeted coaching** (Anthony + Watrous, ~47:10--48:14): underperformers come in for call parties; an hour of content then shift to targeted call work. Adjacent to the call-list playbook, not this page.
- **This is a living doc.** Dan committed to building it out and submitting for revision and approval (~46:40). Treat the page as v1, evolvable.
