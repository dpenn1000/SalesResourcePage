Communities Division · Community Outreach

Build Business Through the People You Already Know

Create opportunities in your own backyard. Build relationships that generate long-term referrals. Support local organizations while you help homeowners, and become the trusted solar resource in your community. Grow your pipeline and strengthen your community at the same time.

Part 1 · The Case

Outreach works because it serves both sides

The best leads come from people and organizations who already trust each other. You meet homeowners through a group they care about, and the group raises money by making the introduction. Everybody wins, and you stand apart from every other solar rep working cold.

What it does for you

  • ✅ Meets homeowners outside traditional sales channels.
  • ✅ Builds credibility through an organization they trust.
  • ✅ Generates referrals from community leaders.
  • ✅ Creates repeat opportunities through events and partnerships.
  • ✅ Sets you apart from other solar companies.

What it does for the organization

  • ✅ Fundraising with no cost to run.
  • ✅ Environmental impact their members care about.
  • ✅ A real member benefit, not another ask.
  • ✅ No cost, no obligation, ever.
"The best leads often come from the people and organizations you already know."
Ask the room: which organization are you already connected to that you have never thought of as a business opportunity?
Recall Why does a community lead beat a cold one?

The homeowner already trusts the organization that introduced you, so you start with credibility instead of resistance. And the organization is motivated to send people your way, because they get paid when a consultation happens.

Part 2 · Your Assignment

Become a Community Ambassador

Over the next two weeks, turn your own network into a partnership pipeline. You are not selling anything yet. You are scouting the groups, events, and people who can open doors.

  1. Research local events. Walks, runs, festivals, and fundraisers already on the calendar near you.
  2. Identify the organizations being supported, and who benefits from them.
  3. Look for aligned groups whose mission fits ours: community, family, environment.
  4. Find the decision-makers, the community leaders who can say yes to a partnership.
  5. Bring opportunities to our next meeting, ready to discuss.

Potential partners are everywhere people gather around a shared cause. Start with the groups you already touch, then work outward across your town.

  • 🚓 First responder organizations
  • 🎭 Community festivals
  • 🏠 Homeowner associations
  • 🙏 Faith-based organizations
  • 🐾 Animal rescue organizations
  • 💙 Local nonprofits
Recall Where is the easiest first partnership?

A group you already belong to or have a personal connection with. Warm beats cold every time, so start with the people who already know you.

Part 4 · Do the Homework

Questions to answer before we meet

A good partnership conversation starts with knowing the organization. Have these answers ready and you will look like a professional, not a solicitor.

  • What organization benefits, and what is its cause?
  • How many members or supporters do they have?
  • What events do they host through the year?
  • Who makes partnership decisions?
  • Do they have newsletters, social media, or a website?
  • Do they actively fundraise?
  • Have they partnered with businesses before?
Ask the room: pick one organization and answer all seven questions out loud. What is still unknown?
